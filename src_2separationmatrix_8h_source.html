11 /// @class SeparationMatrix
12 ///
13 /// This model is intended to represent a generic separations process and is based
14 /// on the separations matrix facility from years past. Based on a matrix of
15 /// incoming streams, outgoing streams, and efficiencies, its purpose is to
16 /// convert a commodity from one commodity to a few after some period of delay
17 /// time. Separations is elemental in this case. This facility is very good for
18 /// use as a separations facility in a reprocessing scenario. It was recently
19 /// re-created to run the fco code-to-code comparison.
20 ///
21 /// The SeparationMatrix class inherits from the Facility class and is
22 /// dynamically loaded by the Agent class when requested.
23 ///
24 /// @section intro Introduction
25 /// For realistic separations, the user is expected to produce an efficiency matrix
26 /// representing the separations technology of interest to them. By requesting the
27 /// feedstock from the appropriate markets, the facility acquires an unseparated
28 /// feedstock stream. Based on the input parameters in Table ref{tab:sepmatrix},
29 /// the separations process proceeds within the timesteps and other constraints of
30 /// the simulation.
31 ///
32 /// Thereafter, separated streams as well as a stream of losses are offered the
33 /// appropriate markets for consumption by other facilities. In the transition
34 /// scenario at hand, the StreamBlender fuel fabrication facility purchases the
35 /// streams it desires in order to produce SFR fuel.
36 ///
37 /// @section agentparams Agent Parameters
38 /// Input Commodity
39 /// An ElementGrouping map
40 ///
41 /// @section optionalparams Optional Parameters
42 ///
43 /// @section detailed Detailed Behavior
44 /// TICK
45 /// Make offers of separated material based on availabe inventory.
46 /// If there are ordersWaiting, prepare and send an appropriate
47 /// request for spent fuel material.
48 /// Check stocks to determine if there is capacity to produce any extra material
49 /// next month. If so, process as much raw (spent fuel) stock material as
50 /// capacity will allow.
51 ///
52 /// TOCK
53 /// Send appropriate separated material from inventory to fill ordersWaiting.
54 ///
55 /// RECIEVE MATERIAL
56 /// Put incoming spent nuclear fuel (SNF) material into stocks
57 ///
58 /// SEND MATERIAL
59 /// Pull separated material from inventory based on Requests
60 /// Decrement ordersWaiting
